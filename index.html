Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Dr. Norman Borlaug, born in 1914 in Cresco, Iowa, was an agronomist and humanitarian known for his significant contributions to agriculture and hunger alleviation. Throughout his life, he played a vital role in revolutionizing wheat production and saved countless lives through his work.

Timeline of Dr. Borlaug's Life

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1933 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to a Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration"
  • 2009 - Dies at the age of 95.
